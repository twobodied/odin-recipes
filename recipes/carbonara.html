Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a creamy pasta dish traditionally made without cream.
A combination of perfectly emulsified eggs and pasta water is used to achieve the creamy texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound pancetta, guanciale or thick cut bacon diced
  • 1 to 2 garlic cloves, minced, approx. 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • 3 to 4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  1. Boil enough water to fully submerge spaghetti. Salt water to taste, (approximately as salty as the ocean).
  2. Heat a pan to medium-high heat and add olive oil, pancetta and garlic to it. Wait until browned, then set aside.
  3. When your water is boiling vigorously, add spaghetti to water then turn down the heat to medium.
  4. Combine your eggs, cheese, pancetta and garlic. Whisk them and season to taste.
  5. When your spaghetti is cooked al dente, save 1/4 cup of pasta water before straining and immediately incorporating into the egg mixture. Mix the spaghetti with tongs to fully cover it in the mixture.
  6. Slowly add the pasta water while you mix to develop the consistency of the sauce.
  7. Once the sauce has thickened, serve the pasta simply with more grated cheese and freshly ground black pepper.
